Matthew Fontaine Maury

14 January 1806 — 1 February 1873

Pathfinder of the Seas • Father of Oceanography

strata.maury

The Pathfinder's Principle

"There is a river in the ocean. In the severest droughts it never fails, and in the mightiest floods it never overflows." — Matthew Fontaine Maury, on the Gulf Stream

No single source tells the complete story. Only by aggregating observations from many sources can we produce charts worthy of the landscape they represent. I collected ship logs from captains across the world—thousands of individual observations—and transformed them into comprehensive wind and current charts that no single voyage could produce.

The maury module embodies this principle: coordinate many sources to reveal patterns invisible to individuals.

Timeline

1806 — Born in Spotsylvania County, Virginia
1826-1830 — First world circumnavigation aboard USS Vincennes
1839 — Stagecoach accident; permanently disabled, unfit for sea duty
1842 — Appointed Superintendent of Naval Observatory, Washington D.C.
1847 — Published first Wind and Current Charts; reduced voyage times by 25-50%
1853 — Organized Brussels Maritime Conference—first international scientific conference
1855 — Published Physical Geography of the Sea—first oceanography textbook
1873 — Died in Lexington, Virginia; buried at Hollywood Cemetery, Richmond

Connection to Strata

Maury embodies the pipeline orchestration philosophy of strata.maury: coordinate many sources to reveal patterns invisible to individuals.

Just as Maury collected ship logs from captains across the world—aggregating thousands of individual observations into comprehensive wind and current charts—the Maury module orchestrates the entire pipeline:

  • Coordinates data from Census Bureau, Quebec, Canada
  • Manages layer composition and ordering
  • Ensures proper sequence of operations
  • Produces unified outputs from disparate sources

Maury's genius was recognizing that systematic aggregation of many observations reveals patterns invisible in any single source.
