The Principle of Unity

"Nature considered rationally, that is to say, submitted to the process of thought, is a unity in diversity of phenomena; a harmony, blending together all created things." — Alexander von Humboldt, Cosmos

When we subtract water from land, we reveal the true relationship between these phenomena—the way the lake carves its space from the surrounding towns. These are not merely shapes but connections made visible. The humboldt module transforms raw geographic data into meaningful maps through geometric operations that reveal hidden patterns.

My greatest insight was that isolated data points become meaningful only through comparison and combination. The same principle applies to cartographic processing: individual shapefiles become maps only through transformation.

Connection to Strata

Humboldt embodies the transformation and processing philosophy of strata.humboldt: seek connections and reveal unity in diversity.

Just as Humboldt transformed raw observations (temperatures, elevations, plant species) into isotherms, altitude profiles, and biogeographic zones—revealing hidden patterns—the Humboldt module transforms raw geographic data into meaningful maps through:

  • Geometric operations (subtract, merge, dissolve)
  • Spatial analysis (merge_touching for cross-border features)
  • Coordinate transformations
  • Topology simplification
